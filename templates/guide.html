Platform Guide

Understanding the building blocks of AIJack. Explore our core data types and how they help you navigate the AI landscape.

Core Entity

AI Tools

The fundamental unit of our platform. An AI Tool represents a specific software, application, or service that utilizes Artificial Intelligence to perform tasks.

Key Data Points:

  • Pricing Model: Free, Freemium, Paid, or Contact for Pricing.
  • Verification: Verified tools (Blue Check) have been manually tested by our team.
  • Affiliation: Direct links to official websites, sometimes with affiliate tracking to support us.
  • Categorization: Tagged by use case (e.g., "Copywriting", "Image Gen") and profession.
Browse All Tools

Anatomy of a Tool Page

  • Header Card: Quick stats (Pricing, Verified/Featured status), Tags, and direct "Visit Website" affiliation link.
  • Visuals: High-res screenshots or video demos to preview the interface.
  • Deep Dive Tabs: Switch between "Overview" for specs and "Use Cases" for practical examples.

User Context

Professions

Targeted collections of tools tailored for specific job roles. We curate the vast AI landscape to show you clearly: "What can I actually use in my job?"

Why browse by Profession?

  • Relevance: Filter out noise. An Accountant doesn't need 3D modeling tools.
  • Discovery: Find niche tools you didn't know existed for your industry.
  • Stacks: See pre-built workflows used by top professionals in your field.
Find Your Profession

Anatomy of a Profession Page

  • Hero Context: Clear icon and tagline defining the role (e.g., "AI Tools for Architects").
  • Smart Filters: Toggle between Free, Freemium, and Paid tools to fit your budget.
  • Recommended Stacks: Curated tool combos specific to this profession's common workflows.

Workflows

Tool Stacks

Why use just one tool? Stacks are powerful combinations of AI tools designed to solve complex, multi-step problems. They represent a complete workflow or a "tech stack" for a specific task.

Stack Features:

  • Community Created: Users can build and share their own successful combinations.
  • AI Generated workflows: Our AI Stack Builder can suggest optimized workflows for your needs.
  • One-Click Save: Save entire workflows to your profile for easy access later.
Explore Stacks

Anatomy of a Stack Page

  • Stack Overview: Tool count, author, and visibility status.
  • AI Workflow Integration: A generated step-by-step guide explaining exactly how to chain these tools together for maximum output.
  • Tools List: The specific set of tools included, with quick links to their individual detail pages.

Physical AI

AI Robots

Going beyond software. This section tracks the rapidly evolving world of embodied AI, including humanoid robots, industrial automations, and consumer robotics.

Tracking The Future:

  • Specs & Details: Track DoF (Degrees of Freedom), height, weight, and battery life.
  • Manufacturer Profiles: Detailed pages for companies like Boston Dynamics, Tesla, and more.
  • Status Updates: Know what's a Concept, Prototype, or Available for Purchase.

Anatomy of a Robot Page

  • Tech Specs Table: Detailed breakdown of physical attributes like Height, Weight, DoF (Degrees of Freedom), and Battery Life.
  • Comparison Tool: Add robots to a side-by-side comparison matrix to evaluate specs against competitors.
  • Software Ecosystem: See which AI models and software tools are compatible with the hardware.
